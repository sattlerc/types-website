\chapter{Preface}

The TYPES meetings are a forum to present new and ongoing work in all
aspects of type theory and its applications, especially in formalized
and computer-assisted reasoning and computer programming. In the
spirit of workshops, talks may be based on newly published papers,
work submitted for publication, but also work in progress.

This year, 69 abstracts were submitted, and each submission was
reviewed by at least three program committee members.
62 abstract were accepted for short presentations (20 minute slots), of which 2 were withdrawn before the conference.
The programme was complemented by five long invited presentations (60 minute slots):
András Kovács (Chalmers University of Technology and University of Gothenburg),
Assia Mahboubi (INRIA Nantes),
Clément Pit-Claudel (EPFL),
Emily Riehl (Johns Hopkins University), and
Daniel Gratzer (Aarhus University).

The conference received financial support from
the Marcus Wallenberg Foundation,
the Chalmers Area of Advance \emph{Information and Communication Technology},
and from Amazon, Jane Street, Formal Vindications and Quantinuum.
We gratefully acknowledge all support.

Similarly to previous years, a formal post-proceedings volume in
Dagstuhl’s \emph{Leibniz International Proceedings in Informatics
  (LIPIcs)} series is planned for after the conference; papers
submitted to that volume need not have been selected for presentation
at the conference, but must represent unpublished work, and are
subjected to (another) full peer-review process.
